# Uganda Zero-Dose Evidence Used by the Prototype

## Rapid Assessment

The Uganda Learning Hub for Immunisation Equity conducted a rapid assessment between August 2023 and March 2024 in Wakiso, Mubende, and Kasese, three districts selected for their high burden of zero-dose children.

The assessment used DHIS2 data, RED/REC access and utilisation categories, zero-dose proportions, district-team engagement, and consideration of equity reference groups to select high-risk communities.

The report is historical evidence and not a live monitoring feed.

## Published Area Signals

The rapid-assessment report describes persistently high zero-dose numbers in:

- Wakiso district: Namugongo Division, Wakiso, and Kyengera Town Council.
- Wakiso house-to-house registration: Kyengera Town Council, Nabweru, and Bweyogerere had high recorded zero-dose numbers.
- Kasese district: Bwera, Isango, and Lake Katwe.

These statements come from the published report's historical analysis. They must not be presented as current conditions without newer validated data.

## Barrier Evidence

Uganda zero-dose studies identify interacting barriers rather than one universal cause. Published examples include:

- Distance and hard-to-reach geography
- Caregiver knowledge gaps
- Fear of side effects
- Cultural or social resistance
- Negative service experiences or health-worker attitudes
- Children born outside health facilities
- Children cared for by non-biological guardians
- Mobile, remote, or underserved populations

The assistant may explain these as documented examples. It must not assign any barrier to a selected area unless the user provides local context or the source explicitly supports that assignment.

## Programme Context

Uganda's National Immunisation Strategy 2024–2028 prioritises:

- Reaching zero-dose, partially vaccinated, and missed communities
- Targeted outreach to hard-to-reach populations
- Closing immunity gaps
- Timely and geographically targeted communication
- Researching, developing, testing, and approving immunisation messages
