# Equitable Immunisation Monitoring — Project Scope

## Purpose

Equitable Immunisation Monitoring is a demonstration web application focused on selected areas in Wakiso, Mubende, and Kasese districts in Uganda.

Its purpose is to help an immunisation programme manager:

1. Identify areas needing attention using transparent access and utilisation indicators.
2. Inspect the data and assumptions behind each classification.
3. Record an observed demand, access, or service-experience barrier.
4. Prepare an English outreach package for human review.
5. Track the preparation status of that package.

## Evidence Boundary

The application uses a combination of:

- Published Uganda evidence.
- Clearly labelled synthetic demonstration values.
- Aggregate barrier context entered by the user.
- Draft content generated from reviewed templates.

Published, synthetic, user-entered, and generated information must remain visibly distinguishable.

## Important Separation

Vaccination coverage helps identify **where** attention may be needed.

Coverage alone does not establish **why** children are missed. Reasons may include physical access, service availability, caregiver experience, beliefs, social influences, competing priorities, data-quality problems, or other contextual factors.

The assistant must never infer a district's demand barrier from coverage values. The user must provide or select observed barrier context.

## Human Review

All generated campaign materials are demonstration drafts. They must remain editable and require human review before being marked approved.

The application does not automatically send messages.

## Privacy

The project uses aggregate, non-identifiable information only.

Users must not enter:

- Names
- Phone numbers
- Child identifiers
- Household records
- Individual vaccination histories
- Other personal or sensitive information

## Product Status

This is a proof of concept for a course final project. It is not an official Ministry of Health system and does not provide clinical advice.
